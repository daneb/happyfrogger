History is not a series of accidents. Scripture presents it as a programme — a purpose conceived before the foundation of the world and carried, without panic, toward its appointed end.

## A purpose, not a reaction

God is never improvising. The fall did not catch heaven by surprise; redemption was not a rescue plan stitched together after the fact. From the beginning there is a settled intention to gather a people and to glorify a Son.

## A plan unfolding in time

The plan moves through covenants — promises made and kept across generations. Each one advances the story: a seed, a nation, a king, a kingdom that cannot be shaken.

## A timeline with an end

Because there is a programme, there is direction. We are not wandering. The same God who began the work has bound himself to complete it.

> To know that history has an Author is to be freed from the tyranny of the headlines.

This chapter is the backbone of the book. Once you see the programme, the individual stories of Scripture stop looking like fragments and start looking like chapters in one book.
