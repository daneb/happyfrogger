There is a quiet crisis that rarely makes the headlines. It is not loud, and it does not arrive all at once. It is the slow absence of the ordinary, faithful man — present at the table, present in prayer, present in the small and unglamorous work of raising a family in the fear of God.

## What absence costs

A home does not collapse in a day. It erodes. When a father is absent — in body, in attention, or in conviction — the people around him learn to live without the thing they most needed. Children adapt. They always do. But adaptation is not the same as flourishing.

Scripture never treats fatherhood as optional decoration. It treats it as stewardship. The man is called to be a picture, however cracked, of a Father who never leaves and never forsakes.

## The ordinary is the point

We tend to imagine that significance lies in the extraordinary. But the Bible keeps pointing us back to the ordinary: the daily bread, the evening prayer, the patient instruction "when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way."

> The most radical thing a man can do today is simply to be there — faithfully, repeatedly, unremarkably there.

## Where we are going

This book is a memoir and a map. In the chapters that follow we will get oriented in God's Word, walk through the journey of salvation, and face the challenges that come after. But it begins here, with presence — because everything else is built on it.
