Most people do not struggle to read the Bible. They struggle to read it *well*. The words are in front of us; the meaning feels just out of reach. The difference is rarely intelligence — it is method.

## Read in context

A verse is not a fortune cookie. It sits inside a paragraph, a chapter, a book, and a whole unfolding story. The first question is never "what does this mean to me?" but "what did this mean to the people who first received it?"

Context is the lock; everything else is the key.

## Let Scripture interpret Scripture

The Bible is its own best commentary. When a passage is hard, the answer is often a few pages — or a few books — away. Clear texts illuminate unclear ones, not the other way around.

## Slow down

We read everything quickly now, and we have trained ourselves to skim. But Scripture rewards the slow reader: the one who notices the repeated word, the sudden shift, the question left hanging.

> Understanding is not downloaded. It is cultivated, like a garden, over seasons.

Pick up the right tools — context, comparison, and patience — and the text begins to open.
