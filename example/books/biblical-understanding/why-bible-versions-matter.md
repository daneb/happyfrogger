Ask which translation someone reads and you will often learn more than you expected — about their tradition, their habits, and what they think a translation is *for*.

## Translation is interpretation

There is no such thing as a neutral translation. Every choice — word order, vocabulary, what to make explicit — is a small act of interpretation. This is not a scandal; it is simply the nature of moving meaning between languages.

## Why I read the KJB

For my own study I read the King James Bible. Its cadence has shaped the English language for four centuries, and its careful, formal approach keeps me close to the structure of the original. That is a personal conviction, not a test of fellowship.

## Read with charity

Whatever version sits on your desk, read it prayerfully and compare widely. The goal is never to win an argument about translations. The goal is to hear God speak.

> A translation is a window, not the view. Keep the glass clean and look through it.

With this chapter, Part I is complete. We have learned to be present, to read well, to see God's programme, and to handle the text with care. The journey of salvation is next.
