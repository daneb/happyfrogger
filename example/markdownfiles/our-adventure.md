We called it an adventure mostly to convince the children, but the name stuck because it turned out to be true.

## The plan

There wasn't one. A map half-folded in the glovebox, a flask of coffee going cold, and the vague intention to follow the coast until we ran out of road or daylight.

## What we found

Not the views — though they were there — but the smaller things. The youngest naming every dog. The argument that dissolved into laughter. The way time slows down when nobody is checking a screen.

> The best adventures are rarely the ones you photograph. They are the ones you remember.

We came home tired and sunburnt and a little more like a family than when we left. That, it turns out, was the whole point.
